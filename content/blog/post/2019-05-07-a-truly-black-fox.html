As a Firefox user at night, I find Dark Reader and Shadowfox real MVPs of the game.

The former is pretty smart at turning websites into dark mode after dynamically analyzing the web-site colors and background images. You can also use shortcut like Option/Alt⌥ + Shift⇧ + D to toggle the extension.

The latter does what Firefox should have included in its so-called dark mode: global dark mode. In this way, the user interface and other “protected” web pages will also be darkened.

Why dark mode? Here are two articles talking about the reasons behind:

  • The Benefits of Dark Mode
  • Reddit: Why do people want dark mode so badly?

I love the top comment in that reddit post:

Night is dark. Screen is bright. Eyes hurt.
Night is dark. Screen is dark. Eyes not hurt.

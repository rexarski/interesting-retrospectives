Ever since Valve updated their privacy policy on Steam, third party tools like SteamSpy would not be able to scrape gaming data from users. It seems that the myth of how many games are there now might stay in the fog forever.

However, Tyler Glaiel on Medium came out with a method to estiamte the Steam game user numbers by analyzing the games’ achievements. In this method, a csv file of a total number of 13281 games was generated.

Soon, Mike Bostock, the creator of D3.js posted a visualization of this dataset in the subreddit r/dataisbeautiful.

Inspired by his work, and also his previous implementation in D3 gallery, I also sketched a crappy version of this dataset.

I’ll just leave it here. Unfortunately, I haven’t figured out how to insert a live version of d3 to a webpage.
